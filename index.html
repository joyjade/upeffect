Funding for social enterprises is broken and it needs a rethink.

Banks perceive social entrepreneurs, BAME, those with disabilities, immigrants, female founders, and those with limited credit histories as high risk.

In 2018, only 2% of venture capital went to female founders and less than 0.2% went to founders of colour (Diversity VC). And business loan approval rates for women are 15-20% lower than men.

The case for crowfunding...

Founders invest substantial time and capital into building and marketing their brands.

Crowdfunding can propel these efforts through the act of raising small amounts of money from a large group of people in exchange for goods and services. This helps founders access customers, funds and partners who join early and become invested in the success of the brand.

But crowdfunding is difficult to navigate.

64% of crowdfunding campaigns fail.

Mainstream crowdfunding platforms are venture-backed. They are built to serve those with existing capital, networks and expertise. Overlooked founders often don’t have access to these resources and are left to fend for themselves while competing with thousands of live campaigns.

UpEffect emerged as a solution to help companies find success and serve society in kinder ways.
